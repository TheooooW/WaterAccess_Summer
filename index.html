Is the water you are swimming inCLEAN?

this is our project, Water Access and Cholera Cases in the Philippines wanting to find out the accessibility of clean water to the Filipinos in each region and how it relates to the number of cases of cholera per region throughout the years. By this information the group will find out the region in where water access is unsufficient and cases of cholera in which may help the government prioritize their projects.

Here's an overview of our project.

This project would like to assesss if there is a relationship between water access and cholera cases in the Philippines.

Motivation

Clean water is one of the most important need of a human to survive in their daily lives. Water is a basic human right and everyone should have access to clean water, but not all people have it.

Problem

Without water, a human may only survive for up to 3 days (WHO, 2021), while having no access to clean water, people result to drinking unclean/unfiltered water and one of the disease they can get is deadly disease Cholera. Vibrio cholerae is the bacteria that causes it which lives in unsanitized/unclean waters and warm environment which is common in the Philippines (CDC, 2023).

Solution

With that, the group will conduct a research on using the data collected to find out the relationship of water access and cholera cases through each region throughout the years, giving a visualization on how severe the need to improve the clean water access to the Filipinos.

Hypothesis

NH : There is no relationship between the water access to the cases of cholera.

AH: There is a negative relationship between water access to the number of cases of cholera (higher clean water access percentage = lower cases of cholera).

Research Questions we wish to find out

Primary Question: How does the accessibility of clean water source to the population relate to cases of cholera per region?

Secondary Question: What is the trend of the access of clean water source per region in the Philippines?

Hypothesis Testing

NH : There is no relationship between the water access to the cases of cholera.
AH: There is a negative relationship between water access to the number of cases of cholera (higher clean water access percentage = lower cases of cholera).

The researchers used correlation in testing the hypothesis, gaining a negative correlation will result to accepting the alternate hypothesis and reject the null hypothesis.

While a positive result or a zero result would otherwise have no evidence to reject the null hypothesis.

Discussion

>The researchers visualized the correlation between the percentage of clean water access and the number of Cholera cases per region in 2020 with the collected data from the Philippine Statistics Authority for the number of cholera cases per region and water access of households per municipality in 2022 from HumData by OHCA.

By separating the data on household access to Clean and Unclean water access, the researchers represented the percentage of clean against unclean water access using a bar graph. The graph shows that Region 4-B and Region-9 have the lowest clean water percentage at 67% and NCR exhibits the highest at 93% clean water access.

The results indicate differences in water access between rural and urban regions, with urban areas having higher percentages of clean water access. The researchers also used a scatter plot to examine the relationship between cholera cases and the percentage of clean water access per Region. The plot shows a positive correlation between the number of cholera cases and clean water access.Furthermore, Region IV-A shows over 200 cases of Cholera, followed by Region VI, suggesting that there is an upward trend relationship between cholera and the percentage of clean water access per region.

Upon further inspection, Region IV-A has the highest number of households with access to water followed by NCR. However, there is a significant difference in the number of cholera cases between the two regions. Therefore, the inferred relationship between the number of cholera cases and water access per region might be spurious.

Conclusion and Recomendation

Clean Water does not mean Cholera-Safe

The clean water access needs to be improved in the Philippines. In some regions, only 67% of households have access to clean water, which is very low, especially for a basic necessity. This research found a positive correlation between water access and the number of cholera cases. This indicates that there is another underlying issue that cannot be solved solely by providing clean water to every household. This pushes future studies to focus on more marine life and the biology of Vibrio Cholerae in the Philippines to reduce the incidence of cholera in the Philippines.

Additionally, there is a clear disparity between the clean water access of regions. This calls for the government to allocate more budget and projects to improve clean water access, proper water disposal, and water irrigation to rural areas, especially in Region 4 and 9, which has the lowest percentage of clean water access and cholera cases are prevalent.

With the current data, the researchers realized a limitation: the current model cannot account for the relationship between the number of cholera cases per region and clean water access per region. Future research could explore additional variables such as demographics, education, assets, and housing conditions. Incorporating these factors might provide more insights into what causes cholera cases in the Philippines.

A recommended project for the government is that water areas that is accessible by the people (ponds, lakes, rivers, etc.), should have atleast a monthly check up for cleanliness and a microbiology test. This check-up should conclude either to allow people to use the water, or block people from accessing the water source in case of unsafe water conditions or infective bacteria.

Lastly, the study advises Filipinos to be wary in the water that comes into contact especially in places where there are outbreaks of Cholera, advising to always check on the news, be mindful of the water you use especially when it comes from unhealthy sources, and to always report on cases of illness and sickness immediately as these may cause an outbreak and infect more people which we do not want.
